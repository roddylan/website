Thermodynamics

Physics

Thermodynamics is the study of the effects of work, heat, and energy on a system. Thermodynamics is only concerned with large scale observations. In science 10 physics, we don't talk very much about thermodynamics, however we do acknowledge 2 (out of 4) of the Laws of Thermodynamics.

The First Law

Energy can't be created or destroyed, but can be transformed/converted from one form to another or transferred from one object to another.

This law is also known as the Law of Conservation of Energy and is very simple. It states that there is a finite amount of energy within the universe that, while it can be converted or transferred, you can't have more or less than what you already have.

The Second Law

No process can be 100% efficient, some energy will always be lost, usually as thermal energy.

This law is also known as entropy, and is also very simple to understand.

It should be noted that in Science 10, you will always consider forces like friction and air resistant as non-existant, and will believe that almost every, if not every, process is 100% efficient despite the second law of thermodynamics.
